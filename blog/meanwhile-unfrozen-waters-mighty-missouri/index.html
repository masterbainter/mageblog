Upon this Tenth Day of January in the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand and Twenty-Six, the frozen waters of the Northern Realms call to those brave enough to answer! Mine intelligence network reports most excellent ice conditions upon the glacial lakes of northeastern Dakota, where the frozen surfaces of Bitter, Enemy Swim, Roy, and Pickerel yield forth perch and walleye to those hardy souls who drill through the crystalline barriers. Even further north in the Minnesota territories, the legendary Upper Red Lake awakens with walleye whilst Mille Lacs offers perch so abundant that entire ice fortresses are being deployed across the frozen expanse!

Meanwhile, upon the unfrozen waters of the mighty Missouri River and her reservoir dominions, the fishing arts continue unabated, though I counsel all who venture forth to consult with the Game Fish and Parks ministry for current conditions. For those who prefer terrestrial pursuits, let it be known that the Season of the Coyote continues with great vigor across our Kingdom, requiring no license nor bureaucratic permission, a true testament to the freedoms we hold dear! Three hundred and fifty-five days remain for this noble hunt.
