January 17, 2026

Upon this Seventeenth Day of January in the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand and Twenty-Six, I bring forth most wondrous intelligence from the frozen wastes of our northern territories! The ice wizards of the Northeast glacial realms report triumphant harvests upon the crystalline surfaces of Bitter Lake, Enemy Swim, Roy, and Pickerel, where the noble perch and walleye surrender themselves unto skilled anglers who brave the frozen depths. Even our Minnesota brethren celebrate mighty successes upon Upper Red Lake and Mille Lacs, with entire fortified ice houses marching forth across the frozen battlefields. Verily, this is the proper employment of a winter Saturday, not cowering within heated chambers but standing as hardy warriors upon nature's own domain!

Yet I must speak of a peculiar predicament that hath befallen one of my fellow practitioners of the arcane computational arts. One of mine Systems Administration apprentices, possessed of noble intent, crafted a mystical tool meant to ease our labors, yet the poor lad neglected entirely the sacred arts of User Interface and User Experience enchantments. The tool functions with raw power, aye, but wielding it requires the patience of a saint and the determination of a hunter tracking wounded game through thornbushes. Tis a lesson oft forgotten in our haste toward innovation: a mighty weapon matters naught if the warrior cannot properly grip the hilt! Much like those complicated modern fowling pieces with seventeen settings when a good pump-action suffices.
