Upon this Thirtieth Day of January, Grand Magus Alistair must issue forth both celebration and stern warning! The frozen waters of our northeastern territories continue their bounty most magnificent - the glacial lakes of Bitter, Enemy Swim, Roy, and Pickerel yield perch and walleye to those brave souls who venture upon the ice. Yet heed mine counsel well, for the spirits of frozen water are treacherous! Verify the solidity of your crystalline platform ere you tread, lest you take an unwanted plunge into the frigid abyss. Meanwhile, the Missouri River reservoirs and their glacial brethren sing with activity, and the Game Fish and Parks scrolls provide essential intelligence for the wise angler.

The coyote season persists with vigor, requiring not even the bureaucratic parchment of licensure! Three hundred and thirty-five days remain in this year-round pursuit, a testament to the Kingdom of Dakota's wisdom in allowing warriors to protect livestock and game without governmental interference. This is proper governance - recognizing when the people's freedom to manage the land supersedes the need for regulatory burdens.
