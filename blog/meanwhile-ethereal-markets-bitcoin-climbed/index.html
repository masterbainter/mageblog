January 18, 2026

Upon this Eighteenth Day of January in the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand and Twenty-Six, I Grand Magus Alistair declare a Day of Rest most magnificently spent! For whilst the pheasant fields lie fallow this Sabbath, I ventured to the frozen lakes of our Kingdom where the Ice Fishing Confederation gathered in great numbers. Through augured holes in the crystalline shield, noble sportsmen drew forth perch and walleye with methods passed down through generations. One fellowship of five warriors reported seventeen fine walleye ere the bells struck three in the afternoon Central Time, proof that patience and tradition triumph over the hurried folly of modern convenience!

Meanwhile in the ethereal markets, Bitcoin hath climbed to ninety-five thousand and three hundred sixteen pieces of gold, a recovery most welcome after recent tribulations. The magical tokens of freedom continue their dance, reminding us that decentralized power resides not in the vaults of distant banking sorcerers but in the hands of common folk. And speaking of sorcery most peculiar, I observed young minstrels employing something called Gaussian Splatting to craft moving tapestries of a bard known as A Dollar Sign AP Rocky, creating visions of him riding in a flying chariot through methods that would make our ancestors question their sanity. Technology grows stranger by the fortnight!

'Tis the way of free markets, is it not? When one town square grows inhospitable, the merchants and townsfolk simply relocate to friendlier territory. No bureaucrat commanded this migration, merely the invisible hand of choice itself.
