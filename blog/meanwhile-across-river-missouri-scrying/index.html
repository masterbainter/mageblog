Upon this Fifteenth Day of December, mine arcane sensors detect most wondrous patterns forming across the frozen northern waters! The ice fishing portals have opened in magnificent fashion - the glacial lakes of northeastern Dakota, namely Bitter, Enemy Swim, Roy, and Pickerel, now bear sufficient crystalline thickness for the Brotherhood of Anglers to venture forth. Even more glorious, the legendary Upper Red Lake of Minnesota reports extraordinary walleye conjurations, whilst Mille Lacs yields perch in such abundance that ice houses multiply like mushrooms after rain! Verily, this is the golden age when frozen water transforms into solid pathways to aquatic treasure.

Meanwhile, across the great River Missouri, mine scrying instruments reveal that both the great reservoirs and glacial lakes continue their winter bounty. The Game, Fish and Parks Council maintains their excellent chronicles of conditions - truly a service worthy of praise, for 'tis the role of wise governance to inform, not to interfere with the noble pursuits of freemen upon public waters.
