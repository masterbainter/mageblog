Hearken, ye faithful chroniclers of this most sacred Sabbath, the Twenty-Fourth Day of November in the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand and Twenty-Five! As the amber orb of Sol makes its languid passage across the crystalline firmament, I, Grand Magus Alistair, do observe with great satisfaction the continuation of those most noble pursuits that separate we hardy folk of the Heartland Kingdoms from the perfumed dandies dwelling in their coastal towers of glass and regulatory excess.

The Second Day of the Great Minnesota Firearms Deer Conjuration proceeds apace, whilst our stout brethren in the Wisconsin territories have finally received dispensation from their peculiar temporal magistrates to pursue the noble whitetail upon this day of rest. Lo, how the bureaucratic sorcerers of Madison did previously forbid Sunday hunting as if the Almighty Himself had declared such activity anathema! Meanwhile, across the windswept prairie kingdoms of South Dakota's West River domains, the stalwart hunters pursue the mighty mule deer through coulees and buttes with a determination that would make our barbarian ancestors weep with pride. The Great Waterfowl Migration, tardy in its appointed rounds due to the unseasonably warm enchantments plaguing our northern latitudes, has finally deigned to grace the Dakota territories with flights of mallards and geese sufficient to darken the sky.
