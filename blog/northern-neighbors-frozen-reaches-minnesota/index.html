Upon this Sixth Day of January in the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand and Twenty-Six, mine heart soars with news of fresh opportunities for the huntsmen of our realm! The Coyote Season hath commenced across the Kingdom of Dakota, and unlike the bureaucratic entanglements that plague other pursuits, this noble hunt requires no license nor permission from distant authorities. Verily, 'tis a reminder that some freedoms remain sacred in these lands, where a man may defend his livestock and test his marksmanship against these cunning beasts throughout the entire year. The predator knows no season, and neither should the skilled hunter!

From our northern neighbors in the frozen reaches of Minnesota, word arrives that the ice grows thick and the fish bite fierce. The great walleye of Upper Red Lake stir beneath their crystalline ceiling, whilst Mille Lacs yields perch of legendary proportion. Mine brethren transport their enchanted houses across the frozen waters, demonstrating the superior wisdom of those who embrace winter rather than flee to warmer, overtaxed southern realms. Such hardy souls understand that prosperity comes not from avoiding hardship, but from mastering it with proper equipment and ancestral knowledge.
